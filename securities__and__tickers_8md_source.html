00001 # ITCH-5.0: Confused about Securities, Symbols, and Tickers {#securities-vs-symbols}
00002 
00003 The ITCH-5.0 protocol suffers from a common confusion in the
00004 securities industry.  It calls their ticker field 'Stock'.  While it
00005 is true that most securities traded on Nasdaq are common stock
00006 securities, many are not: Nasdaq also trades Exchange Traded Funds,
00007 Exchange Traded Notes, and even Warrants.
00008 
00009 And the name of a security, i.e., the string used to identify the
00010 security in the many electronic protocols used between exchanges and
00011 participants, is not the same as the security.  A more appropriate
00012 name would have been 'Security Identifier', or 'Ticker', or 'Symbol'.
00013 
00014 In software we are used to not confusing an object with the multiple
00015 references to it.
00016 Likewise, we should get used to not confuse stock, which is a
00017 security, that is, a contract granting specific rights to its owner;
00018 vs. the name of the thing, such as a ticker: a short string used to
00019 identify a security in some contexts.
00020 
00021 Also, software engineers in the field should be aware that the same
00022 security may be identified by different strings in different contexts,
00023 e.g. many exchanges used different tickers for the same security, yes,
00024 even in the US markets.
00025 This is particularly obvious if you start
00026 looking at securities with suffixes, such as preferred stock.
00027 In addition, the same tickers refers to
00028 different securities as time goes by, for example, after some
00029 corporate actions.
00030 
00031 And outside the US markets it is common to use fairly
00032 long strings (ISINs) or just numbers (in Japan) to identify
00033 securities in computer systems.
00034 And that the string used to identify securities in a
00035 market feed may not be the same string used to identify them when
00036 placing orders, or clearing.
00037 
00038 In short, it behooves software engineers in the field to keep these
00039 things straight in their designs, and in their heads too I might add.
